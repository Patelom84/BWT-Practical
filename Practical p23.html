Indus Valley Civilization: This highly organized culture, originating in the Indus River valley (modern-day Pakistan and northwest India), flourished from about 2500 to 2000 BCE. It was characterized by sophisticated urban planning, advanced drainage systems, standardized weights, and a written script (yet to be deciphered).

Vedic Period: Following the decline of the Indus Valley Civilization, Indo-Aryan tribes migrated into the Punjab from Central Asia, marking the beginning of the Vedic period (c. 1500–500 BCE). During this era, the extensive collections of hymns known as the Vedas were composed, laying the groundwork for Hindu traditions.

Mauryan Empire: In the 3rd century BCE, Ashoka, the first to unify India under a single rule, converted to Buddhism and helped spread the religion throughout Asia.

Arrival of Islam:
Islam first came to India in the 8th century CE, with a significant political presence established by the 11th century. This led to the formation of the Delhi Sultanate, a precursor to the Mughal Empire.

Mughal Empire: This empire brought a large measure of political unity to India and was in place when Europeans first arrived in the 17th century.

European Influence Decline of the Mughals and Rise of Regional States: The disintegration of the Mughal Empire coincided with the arrival of Europeans, creating a power vacuum filled by regional states.
