Computer

A computer is an electronic device that processes and stores data, performing tasks based on a set of instructions called a program.
How a computer works The fundamental work flow of a computer is often referred to as the "Input-Process-Output" cycle. Input: A user provides raw data and instructions through input devices, such as a keyboard, mouse, or microphone. The computer then converts this information into a binary format (1s and 0s) that its processor can understand. Processing: The Central Processing Unit (CPU), or "the brain" of the computer, executes the program's instructions by performing calculations and logical operations. Output: After processing the data, the computer presents the results to the user through output devices like a monitor, speakers, or printer. Storage: The computer can store data and instructions for future use in both temporary (RAM) and permanent (hard drive or SSD)0 memory.

Cricket

Cricket is a popular bat-and-ball sport played between two teams of 11 players on a field with a 22-yard pitch at the center. The game's objective is for the batting team to score runs while the fielding team attempts to restrict runs and dismiss the batters.Test cricket: The longest and most traditional form of the game, lasting up to five days. Each team has two innings to bat and bowl, emphasizing strategy and endurance. Players wear traditional white attire.

music

Music is a universal art form that uses vocal or instrumental sounds arranged in combinations of rhythm, melody, and harmony. Present in every human culture, it is a versatile medium for expressing human creativity and emotion. Melody: A coherent and memorable sequence of pitches, often considered the "tune" of a song. Harmony: The simultaneous combination of musical notes to form chords, adding depth and texture to a melody. Harmony can be consonant (pleasant) or dissonant (tense). /
